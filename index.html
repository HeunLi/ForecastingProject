Naive Approach

A simple forecasting technique that assumes future values will be the same as the most recent observed value. It does not consider any underlying patterns, trends, or seasonality in the data. This approach is easy to implement but may not be suitable for datasets with complex patterns or variations.

3-Month Moving Average

A method that calculates the average of the past three months' data points to forecast future values. It smoothes out short-term fluctuations and highlights longer-term trends in the data. This approach is useful for identifying overall patterns and making predictions, but it may lag behind sudden changes or seasonality in the data.

3-Month Weighted Moving Average

A method that assigns different weights to each data point in the past three months, giving more significance to recent observations. This approach allows for capturing more timely changes in the data compared to the simple moving average. However, it requires careful selection of the weights to ensure an appropriate balance between responsiveness and smoothness in the forecast.
